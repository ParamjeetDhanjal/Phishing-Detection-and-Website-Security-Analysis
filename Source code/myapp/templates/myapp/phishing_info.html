What to Do If You Get Phished

1. Disconnect and Stay Calm

Unplug from the internet if sensitive data was entered. Stay calm — quick action helps!

2. Change Passwords

Immediately change the password of the affected account and any others that share the same password.

3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add 2FA to important accounts to add an extra layer of protection.

4. Check for Unauthorized Activity

Review account login activity, emails, banking transactions, and other signs of unauthorized access.

5. Scan Your Device

Use antivirus or anti-malware tools like Malwarebytes to scan for malicious software.

6. Report the Phishing

Report to your email provider, website support, or government agencies:

  • India’s Cyber Crime Portal
  • Google Phishing Report

7. Contact Your Bank

If banking info was shared, call your bank to block cards or monitor for suspicious activity.

8. Educate Yourself

Learn how to identify phishing emails and avoid clicking unknown links. Always verify before trusting.
